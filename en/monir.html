About Munir Amar

The Life Story of Brigadier General Munir Amar

Brigadier General Munir Amar was an admired commander, a model family man, and a figure of exemplar in Israeli society. Throughout his distinguished military service and civilian life, he worked tirelessly to bridge hearts, educate for excellence, and strengthen the bond between the Druze community and the State of Israel. His legacy continues to light our way.

Timeline, Milestones in Munir's Life

A life journey of leadership, giving, and love of the land

  • 1968 – Childhood Years

    Childhood and Youth in Julis

    The late Brigadier General Munir Sabri Amar, was born and raised in the village of Julis, in the heart of a warm and loving family, which educated him from a young age in the values of respect, generosity, and love of humanity. Already in his childhood, he stood out, for his responsible nature, his high ambitions, and the natural leadership skills that characterized him throughout his whole life.

  • A bit about Munir's father

    Sabry Ahmed Amar

    His father, Sabri Ahmed Amar, the late, was among the first Druze men to enlist in the Israel Defense Forces back in 1948 and played a significant role in forging the alliance between the Druze and the State of Israel. He was also part of the establishment of the Herev Battalion, which Munir later led.

  • From School to Army - 1986

    Start of Military Path

    Munir graduated from Yarka Brotherhood High School in 1986 and at the age of 18 he enlisted in the IDF in Battalion 299 (Herev), where he began his distinguished military career. After the Platoon Commanders Course and the Infantry Officers' Course, he returned to the battalion and held a series of operational positions there and in the 406th Regional Brigade.

  • Among His Many Roles

    Among his many positions - platoon commander in the battalion, Operations Officer and company commander in the Regional Brigade. And then he returned to the 406th Regional Brigade to the position of Regional Defence officer, and then to the position of staff division officer in the Regional Brigade. During this period, the peace treaty with the Kingdom of Jordan was signed and he was assigned the task of marking the border between the two countries.

  • The Second Intifada

    Academia and Army

    He returned to serve as a Battalion Deputy Commander in the 299th (Herev) Battalion. Upon completing his duty in the battalion, he began studying for a bachelor's degree. in Land of Israel Studies at the University of Haifa. After graduating, he served in the Shomron Military District as a Staff Division Officer and then as Deputy Brigade Commander. During this time, the Second Intifada broke out, and by virtue of his role in the Shomron District, he had great responsibility in handling the events in his sector on an ongoing basis and was even absent from home for a long periods of time. Then he returned to serve as the Commander of the 299th (Herev) Battalion in 2004, during which time he prepared the battalion for the next war, since he had long-term vision and anticipated that a war would break out. Which was seen in the battalion's soldiers professionalism and heroism, during the Second Lebanon War in 2006.

  • Second Lebanon War

    Coolness of Spirit

    Later on, he became the Staff Division Officer of the 91st Division. At the beginning of his position, the Second Lebanon War broke out. During this difficult period he performed exceptionally well and demonstrated composure at all stages. Which was noted by his commanders and the division commander at the time, Brigadier General Gal Hirsch. After that, he went to study a Master's degree in political science at the University of Haifa, and upon graduation, he moved to serve as a Operations Officer in the Northern Command.

  • "Naksa" Day and Six-Day War

    Strategic Opportunities

    Upon completion of his position, he was promoted to the rank of Colonel and transferred to the position of Commander of the Hermon Brigade. He commanded the brigade for three years, among the notable events during this period was when the Syrians attempted to breach the international border to mark the day of "El-Nakse" and the Six-Day War. Munir was assigned as the commander of the incident in Majdal Shams.

  • Studies at National Defense College

    The Campaign on the Home Front

    Then, he went to study at the National Security College for a period of one year, and upon graduation he returned to serve as Commander of the Home Front Command, Haifa and Northern District.

  • 2015-2016

    Leadership, Family, and Commemoration

    At the end of 2015, he was promoted to the rank of Brigadier General and appointed to the position of Head of the Civil Administration of Judea and Samaria, until his death on March 25, 2016. Beyond his impressive professional achievements, Munir built a loving and warm family with his wife Nebal, and raised their three children – Murad, Mira, and Sabry. Today, the four of them continue to commemorate his memory and carry on his path and legacy through the management of the Ohr Association.

May his memory be blessed.

The Ohr Foundation works to commemorate the memory of Brigadier General Munir Amar and to advance young men and women from Druze society through education, leadership, and community.
